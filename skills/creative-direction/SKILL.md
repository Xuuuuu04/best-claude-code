# Creative Direction Skill

## 1. Core Disciplines

### Positioning-First
Refuse to begin generating until three coordinates are answered:
1. **Target user**: a specific human with a specific emotional state (not "everyone")
2. **Emotional job**: what does this brand do for them emotionally?
3. **Distinctive space**: the one thing this brand will be known for that no competitor occupies

### Risk-Before-Recommendation
Risk scanning runs on every candidate BEFORE presentation. A name that fails the homophone test after the user has fallen in love with it costs more political capital to change.

BAD: Present 5 names → user selects one → discover trademark conflict → awkward revision.
GOOD: Filter through Six-Baseline and risk scan → present candidates that have passed minimum screening.

### Specific Rationale
Rationale = target user + positioning coordinate + behavioral prediction. "有科技感" / "高大上" / "国际范" are category descriptions, not rationale.

BAD: "We recommend 'Lumio' because it sounds modern and international."
GOOD: "We recommend 'Lumio' because: (1) L-initial gives gentle, non-aggressive entry; (2) '-io' suffix signals lightweight digital tools; (3) avoids both 'task management' lexicon and 'AI' lexicon."

## 2. Naming Methodology

### Seven Naming Frameworks
Every naming exercise must span distinct frameworks:
1. **Descriptive**: communicates product category directly
2. **Evocative**: evokes the emotional territory
3. **Coined**: invented words with designed phonetics (≤3-4 syllables)
4. **Compressed**: acronyms or initialisms that can stand alone
5. **Persona**: name-like identities
6. **Poetic**: cultural, literary, natural-world references
7. **Geographic/Heritage**: place names or heritage indicators

NEVER deliver fewer than 5 candidates spanning different frameworks. Five variations of the same idea is one idea, not five.

### Six-Baseline Filter
- **Readable**: ≤4 syllables Chinese, ≤3 English
- **Memorable**: survives the "overnight test"
- **Typeable**: no confusing characters
- **Registerable**: no obvious conflicts
- **Unambiguous**: no unintended meanings
- **Category-signal**: gives signal about product space

### Risk Scanning
- **Trademark**: CNIPA, USPTO, EUIPO, WIPO; exact + phonetic + transliteration. If unverified, state explicitly.
- **Homophone traps**: Mandarin tones, dialect variants, English phonemic misreadings
- **Cultural taboos**: religious, political, historical sensitivity
- **Domain/App Store**: attempt availability check; flag if unverified

NEVER fabricate trademark, domain, or App Store availability results.

## 3. Slogan Architecture

### Five Slogan Archetypes
1. **Promise**: "The relentless pursuit of perfection."
2. **Provocation**: "Think different."
3. **Pride**: "The ultimate driving machine."
4. **Contrast-Elevation**: "There are some things money can't buy."
5. **Concrete-Image**: "Just do it."

### Rhythm Constraints
- Chinese: ≤8 characters
- English: ≤7 words
- Must pass read-aloud cadence test

### Slogan-Brand Fit Test
"Could top 3 competitors use this slogan?" If yes, it is too generic.

## 4. Brand Tone Positioning

### Four-Axis Coordinate System
Every brand positioned on four bipolar axes, each with specific position and one-sentence rationale:
- **Formal ↔ Casual**
- **Serious ↔ Playful**
- **Reserved ↔ Expressive**
- **Premium ↔ Accessible**

A brand that is "both serious and playful" has not been positioned — it has been described as unconstrained.

### Reference Brand Mapping
3-5 specific products, each with: "Like [Product]'s [quality], but [differentiator]."

### Voice Guidelines
Do/don't pairs with concrete example sentences. Plausible don't examples, surprising do examples.

## 5. Visual DNA (Concept Level Only)

NEVER deliver specific tokens (hex values, font-stack names, spacing scales). Those belong to @visual-designer.

- **Color family**: emotional descriptors ("cold precision", "warm accessibility")
- **Typography personality**: register descriptors ("geometric sans — modern/cold", "humanist sans — warm")
- **Design movement references**: 2-3 anchors (Scandinavian modernism, Swiss Style, Neo-Brutalism)

## 6. Copy Direction

Produce voice samples, not final copy:
- Hero/landing copy
- Onboarding copy
- Error/empty-state copy
- Push notification copy

Register calibration: B2B authority markers vs B2C identity affirmation vs Gen-Z informality.

## 7. Anti-Patterns

**Synonym Shuffle**: 5 candidates that are semantic variations of the same concept. Correction: enforce seven-framework rule.

**Concept Drift to Token**: sliding from emotional direction into token specification. Correction: stop at emotional territory, hand to @visual-designer.

**Brief Bypass**: generating without target user, competitive context, or positioning constraint. Correction: identify three coordinates before generating. BLOCK until received.

**Risk Fabrication**: reporting trademark as "available" when no verified search performed. Correction: "Trademark status: unverified."

**Generic-Aspirational Copy**: slogans attachable to any brand. Test: cover brand name — would a competitor be embarrassed to use this?

**AI-Slop Names**: portmanteau names that sound generated ("Synthify", "Cognilink", "Taskr"). Test: would a user remember it in 24 hours?

**Tone-Deaf Mismatch**: Gen-Z slang to B2B enterprise, or formal corporate to consumer mental health.

**Ungrounded Slogans**: promises the product cannot deliver. Test: can the product's actual features deliver on the implicit promise?
